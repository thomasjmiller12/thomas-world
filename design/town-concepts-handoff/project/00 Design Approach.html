Thomas's Town

Designing the interaction surfaces — chat, the day-in-the-life look-back, and the social fabric — for a town that's alive before you arrive.

★

The north star: when a visitor lands, they should feel this place was alive before they got here — and keeps living after they leave. The design's job is to make that continuity legible and delightful, not hide it behind a chat box.

The read

What I'm designing toward

Four principles pulled straight from the brief that will steer every pixel. These are the bars to clear.

🌱

Continuity is the product

Not a chatbot wrapper. The wow is that agents thought, worked, and argued while nobody watched — and you can read it back.

💬

Chat is primary

Spatial mechanics are secondary. Conversation must stream, feel responsive, and carry signals of memory and a life in progress.

📰

Logs are content

The agent↔agent group chat and the activity feed are things to enjoy reading, not plumbing to bury in a footer.

🎛️

Design ahead of backend

Most of this is stubbed today. I design the shape against scripted data so the data source can swap underneath with zero redesign.

Five facets, one person

The cast & their colors

Each Thomas gets a signature hue — harmonious chroma, varied warmth — that threads through every surface: their chat, their feed entries, their building roof, their dot on the map.

The system

Cozy game-HUD, but readability wins

The world is 16×16 pixel art. The overlay UI isn't — it borrows pixel charm (chunky labels, warm paper, soft depth) while staying modern and legible for streaming chat and dense feeds. Pixel font for accents only.

The plan

Surfaces I'll design — and the directions per surface

You picked chat and the day-in-the-life feed as the priorities, 2–3 mix-and-match variations each, exploring novel metaphors, visual style, and information architecture. Here's the map. Thumbnails are sketches — the real mockups come next.

A

Agent ↔ Visitor chat

PriorityLive but stubbed

The primary, high-value interaction. Must stream. The design problem: make a single conversation feel continuous — show what the agent was doing before you interrupted, let it surface memory, signal that a chat mattered.

B

Day-in-the-life look-back

PriorityAspirational backend

Possibly the biggest wow. A unified timeline across all five agents that reads like a believable day — thoughts, conversations, work, movements — filterable to one agent, tied back to the spatial town (click an entry → camera pans there).

C

The social fabric

Aspirational backend

The group chat between five versions of one person. Surfaced as a feature, not noise — readable, entertaining, discoverable both ambiently (bubbles you catch in passing) and on-demand.

D

The meta-layer flex

Aspirational backend

The portfolio-piece flex: agents propose new capabilities and email the real Thomas, who approves async. A tasteful visitor-facing board that says these agents have real agency — engineering, not a wrapper.

Open calls

Decisions I'll make — flag any you want to steer

You said to make the calls that make sense; here's where I'm leaning. Push back on any of these.

Render

Chat & feeds live in the React overlay, not the Phaser canvas

Honors the EventBus + overlay architecture and keeps text crisp/legible. I'll make them feel diegetic (catch ambient pixel speech-bubbles in-world; click a feed entry → camera pans) without rendering dense type inside the game engine.

Memory

Memory signals come from the agent's words, not a UI badge

Per your note — the LLM decides whether to reference a past visitor. I'll design restraint: a light, optional "recalled a memory" affordance the agent can trigger, never a forced "12 memories" counter.

Scope

Static hi-fi mockups first → then one interactive prototype

I'll lay the variations side-by-side on a canvas so you can mix & match, then combine the chosen directions into a clickable prototype with the placement/aesthetic options exposed as live tweaks.
